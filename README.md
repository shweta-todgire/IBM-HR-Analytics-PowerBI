# 📊 IBM HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard** developed in **Microsoft Power BI** using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition dataset. The dashboard helps HR professionals and business leaders analyze workforce trends, employee attrition, salary distribution, career growth, and employee satisfaction to support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition across different business dimensions.
- Identify workforce demographics and attrition trends.
- Evaluate salary and career growth patterns.
- Understand employee satisfaction and work-life balance.
- Provide interactive insights through Power BI visualizations.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- Microsoft Power BI
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Power Query
- IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset

---

## 📂 Dashboard Pages

### 📄 Page 1 – Executive Summary

Provides a high-level overview of the organization's workforce.

#### KPIs
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Job Satisfaction

#### Visualizations
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Gender
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Age Group
- Attrition by Business Travel

---

### 📄 Page 2 – Salary & Career Growth Analysis

Focuses on employee compensation and career progression.

#### KPIs
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Salary Hike
- Average Performance Rating
- Average Years at Company

#### Visualizations
- Average Monthly Income by Department
- Monthly Income by Job Role
- Job Level vs Average Income
- Overtime vs Average Income

- Years Since Last Promotion

### 📄 Page 3 – Employee Satisfaction & Workforce Insights

Analyzes employee engagement and workplace satisfaction.

#### KPIs
- Average Job Satisfaction

#### Visualizations
- Job Satisfaction by Department

- Environment Satisfaction Distribution
- Work-Life Balance by Job Role
- Attrition by Overtime
- Education Field vs Job Satisfaction
- Stock Option Level vs Attrition Rate

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Research & Development experienced the highest employee attrition.
- Employees aged **26–35** showed the highest attrition rate.
- Laboratory Technicians and Sales Executives recorded the highest attrition among job roles.
- Monthly income increased consistently with higher job levels.
- Employees with lower stock option levels experienced higher attrition.
- Work-life balance and job satisfaction remained relatively consistent across departments.
- Business travel and overtime showed noticeable relationships with employee attrition.
